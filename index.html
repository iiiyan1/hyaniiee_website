Facts about Teenage Pregnancy

millions of pregnant teens

In 2019, about 21 million pregnancies occurred annually among girls aged 15â€“19 in low- and middle-income countries, with around 50% being unintended, leading to 12 million births. Over half of these unintended pregnancies end in unsafe abortions, and adolescent mothers face higher health risks like eclampsia and infections, while their babies face low birth weight and preterm birth. Preventing teenage pregnancies is essential for improving maternal and newborn health and achieving Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Health Complications

Pregnancy and childbirth complications are the leading cause of death for girls aged 15 to 19 in developing countries, with about 70,000 adolescent mothers dying yearly due to early pregnancies. Girls in this age group are twice as likely to die from pregnancy-related causes, and their babies face a 50% higher risk of death compared to babies of older mothers. The youngest mothers, aged 10 to 14, have up to five times higher maternal mortality risk than women aged 20 to 24.

financial trouble

Teen mothers often drop out of high school to support themselves and their babies, leading to low-paying jobs and poverty. Many rely on public assistance due to limited education and job opportunities. Investing in teen pregnancy prevention can reduce poverty and save taxpayers millions.
